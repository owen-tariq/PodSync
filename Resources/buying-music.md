# Buying Music

PodCenter can help you find tracks to purchase from online music stores. This is useful when your streaming playlists contain songs you don't yet own locally — buying them lets you add them to your library and sync to your iPod.

> **PodCenter has no affiliate relationships with any music store.** These links are provided purely as a convenience to help you fill out your local library. PodCenter receives no commission, referral fee, or financial benefit of any kind from your purchases.

## How It Works

When you're comparing your streaming playlists against your local library, some tracks won't have a match. PodCenter can open a search for those tracks on supported music stores, right in your browser.

From there, you purchase and download the track through the store's normal process, add it to your library folder, and PodCenter picks it up on the next scan.

## Supported Stores

| Store | What It Offers |
|-------|---------------|
| **Qobuz** | Hi-res and lossless downloads (FLAC up to 24-bit/192kHz). Strong catalog for classical, jazz, and audiophile releases. |
| **Bandcamp** | Artist-direct purchases. Strong for indie, electronic, and niche genres. Downloads in FLAC, ALAC, WAV, MP3, and more. |

## Where to Find It

### Streaming Playlist View

Right-click any track in a streaming playlist to see the **Search Music Stores** submenu. This works for both linked and unlinked tracks.

### Link Review

When matching a streaming playlist to your library, right-click any row — especially "No match found" rows — and choose **Search on Qobuz** or **Search on Bandcamp**.

The candidate review popover also includes store search links at the bottom.

## After Purchasing

Once you've downloaded a track from a store:

1. Move the file into your library folder
2. PodCenter detects the new file automatically
3. Re-run the playlist linking process — the track should now match

If the track doesn't match automatically, you can always link it manually using **Link to Library...** from the right-click menu.

## Tips

- **Qobuz** is a good default for lossless audio quality. FLAC files from Qobuz are automatically converted for iPod compatibility during sync.
- **Bandcamp** often has releases you won't find elsewhere, especially from smaller artists.
- Search results may not always find an exact match — store catalogs vary, and some tracks are exclusives on specific platforms.
- You can always search the stores manually in your browser if the automated search doesn't find what you need.
